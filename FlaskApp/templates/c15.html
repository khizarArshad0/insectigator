Western Corn Rootworms

Dangers They Pose: Western corn rootworms are destructive pests that feed on the roots of corn plants, causing lodging and reduced yield. They can weaken plants and make them more susceptible to lodging and other stressors.

Insecticide: Insecticides containing ingredients such as soil-applied systemic insecticides, seed treatments, and foliar insecticides are commonly used to control western corn rootworm populations.


Preferred Habitats: Western corn rootworms primarily inhabit cornfields and other fields where corn is grown. They may also infest grassy areas, pastures, and overwinter in crop residue.

Lifecycle: Western corn rootworms undergo complete metamorphosis, consisting of egg, larva, pupa, and adult stages. The lifecycle duration varies depending on temperature and food availability, typically ranging from one to two years.

Season They Are Active: Western corn rootworms are most active during the warmer months of the year, typically from late spring to early fall. They may overwinter as eggs in the soil and emerge as larvae in the spring to feed on corn roots.
