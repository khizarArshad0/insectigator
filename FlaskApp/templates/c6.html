Citrus Canker

Dangers They Pose: Citrus canker is a bacterial disease that affects citrus trees, causing lesions on leaves, stems, and fruit. It can lead to defoliation, fruit drop, and reduced fruit quality, ultimately impacting citrus production.

Insecticide: Copper-based fungicides and bactericides are commonly used to control citrus canker, along with cultural practices such as pruning and sanitation.


Preferred Habitats: Citrus canker primarily affects citrus trees, including oranges, lemons, limes, and grapefruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often spread through wind-driven rain and contaminated equipment.

Lifecycle: Citrus canker is caused by the bacterium Xanthomonas citri subsp. citri. It enters citrus trees through wounds or natural openings and multiplies in leaf lesions, leading to the development of characteristic raised lesions surrounded by a water-soaked margin.

Season They Are Active: Citrus canker can be active year-round in regions with suitable climates for citrus cultivation. However, it may be more prevalent during periods of warm, humid weather when conditions are favorable for bacterial growth and spread.
