Spider Mites

Dangers They Pose: Spider mites are tiny arachnids that feed on plant sap, causing damage to a wide range of crops, ornamental plants, and trees. They can weaken plants, reduce yields, and cause leaf discoloration or stippling.

Insecticide: Insecticidal soaps, neem oil, horticultural oils, and miticides are commonly used to control spider mite populations.


Preferred Habitats: Spider mites thrive in warm, dry conditions and are often found on the undersides of leaves. They can infest a variety of plants, including vegetables, fruits, ornamentals, and greenhouse crops.

Lifecycle: Spider mites undergo a simple life cycle, consisting of egg, nymph, and adult stages. They reproduce rapidly, with populations increasing exponentially under favorable environmental conditions.

Season They Are Active: Spider mites are active year-round in temperate climates but may be more prevalent during hot, dry weather. They can become a problem indoors or in greenhouses during winter months when environmental conditions are conducive to their survival.
