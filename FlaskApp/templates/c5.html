Cabbage Loopers

Dangers They Pose: Cabbage loopers are destructive pests that feed on a variety of cruciferous crops, including cabbage, broccoli, and cauliflower. They cause damage by chewing holes in leaves, resulting in reduced crop quality and yield.

Insecticide: Bacillus thuringiensis (Bt) insecticides, spinosad, and pyrethroids are commonly used to control cabbage looper populations.


Preferred Habitats: Cabbage loopers prefer to inhabit gardens, farms, and fields where cruciferous crops are grown. They may also be found in other leafy greens and ornamental plants.

Lifecycle: Cabbage loopers undergo complete metamorphosis, consisting of egg, larva, pupa, and adult stages. The lifecycle duration varies depending on temperature and food availability, typically ranging from 3 to 4 weeks.

Season They Are Active: Cabbage loopers are most active during the warmer months of the year, typically from spring to fall. They may overwinter as pupae in soil or plant debris and emerge as adults when temperatures rise in the spring.
