Brown Marmorated Stink Bugs

Dangers They Pose: Brown marmorated stink bugs can cause damage to a wide range of crops, including fruits, vegetables, and ornamental plants. They feed by piercing plant tissue and sucking out sap, leading to deformed fruit, reduced yields, and cosmetic damage.

Insecticide: Pyrethroid insecticides, neonicotinoids, and botanical insecticides are commonly used to control brown marmorated stink bug populations.


Preferred Habitats: Brown marmorated stink bugs are often found in agricultural areas, gardens, orchards, and urban landscapes. They seek shelter in protected areas such as buildings, homes, and sheds during the winter months.

Lifecycle: Brown marmorated stink bugs undergo incomplete metamorphosis, consisting of egg, nymph, and adult stages. The lifecycle duration varies depending on temperature and food availability, typically ranging from 4 to 12 months.

Season They Are Active: Brown marmorated stink bugs are most active during the warmer months of the year, typically from spring to fall. They become more active in the spring as temperatures rise and continue to be active until temperatures drop in the fall.
