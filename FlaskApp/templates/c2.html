Aphids

Dangers They Pose: Aphids feed on sap from plants, causing damage such as yellowing leaves, stunted growth, and distorted plant tissue. They can also transmit plant diseases.

Insecticide: Neem oil, insecticidal soap, and systemic insecticides are commonly used to control aphid populations.


Preferred Habitats: Aphids prefer to inhabit the undersides of leaves, tender shoots, and new growth on plants, where they can feed on plant sap.

Lifecycle: Aphids reproduce rapidly through parthenogenesis, with females giving birth to live nymphs without mating. They go through multiple molts as nymphs before maturing into winged or wingless adults.

Season They Are Active: Aphids are most active during the spring and summer months when temperatures are warmer and plant growth is vigorous. They can continue to thrive in mild climates throughout the year.
