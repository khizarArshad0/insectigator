Colorado Potato Beetles

Dangers They Pose: Colorado potato beetles are destructive pests that feed on potato plants and related crops, such as tomatoes and eggplants. They cause damage by defoliating plants, leading to reduced yields and crop quality.

Insecticide: Insecticides containing ingredients such as pyrethroids, neonicotinoids, and spinosad are commonly used to control Colorado potato beetle populations.


Preferred Habitats: Colorado potato beetles prefer to inhabit potato fields and gardens where their host plants are grown. They may also be found on other solanaceous crops and weeds.

Lifecycle: Colorado potato beetles undergo complete metamorphosis, consisting of egg, larva, pupa, and adult stages. The lifecycle duration varies depending on temperature and food availability, typically ranging from 3 to 5 weeks.

Season They Are Active: Colorado potato beetles are most active during the warmer months of the year, typically from late spring to early fall. They may overwinter as adults in soil or plant debris and emerge in the spring to mate and lay eggs on host plants.
