Fruit Flies

Dangers They Pose: Fruit flies are nuisance pests that infest ripe or decaying fruits and vegetables, causing contamination and spoilage. They can also transmit bacteria and pathogens, posing a potential health risk.

Insecticide: Fruit fly traps, baits, and insecticides containing ingredients such as spinosad or pyrethrin are commonly used to control fruit fly populations.


Preferred Habitats: Fruit flies are commonly found near fruits, vegetables, and fermenting organic matter. They are attracted to sugary and decaying substances and may also inhabit garbage cans, drains, and compost bins.

Lifecycle: Fruit flies undergo a simple life cycle, consisting of egg, larva, pupa, and adult stages. The lifecycle duration is short, typically ranging from 7 to 14 days under favorable conditions.

Season They Are Active: Fruit flies are active year-round, but they are most prevalent during the warmer months of the year, especially in late summer and early fall when fruits are abundant and temperatures are favorable for their development.
