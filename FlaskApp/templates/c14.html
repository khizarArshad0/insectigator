Tomato Hornworms

Dangers They Pose: Tomato hornworms are large caterpillars that feed on the leaves and fruits of tomato plants and related crops, such as peppers and eggplants. They can cause extensive defoliation and damage to fruit, leading to reduced yields.

Insecticide: Insecticides containing Bacillus thuringiensis (Bt), spinosad, or neem oil are commonly used to control tomato hornworm populations.


Preferred Habitats: Tomato hornworms prefer to inhabit tomato fields, vegetable gardens, and other solanaceous plants. They may also be found on related weeds and wild host plants.

Lifecycle: Tomato hornworms undergo complete metamorphosis, consisting of egg, larva, pupa, and adult stages. The lifecycle duration varies depending on temperature and food availability, typically ranging from 4 to 6 weeks.

Season They Are Active: Tomato hornworms are most active during the warmer months of the year, typically from late spring to early fall. They may overwinter as pupae in soil and emerge as adults in the spring to mate and lay eggs on host plants.
